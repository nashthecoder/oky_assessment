Two test articles used to demonstrate the urban/rural content filter in the CMS and app. One targets rural users only; the other is visible to everyone.

Article 1 — Rural only

Category Health
Subcategory Period pain
Article Title Managing period pain without a pharmacy nearby
Article Content If you live far from a pharmacy or clinic, there are safe ways to ease period pain at home.

Try placing a warm cloth or heated water bottle on your lower belly — warmth relaxes the muscles and can reduce cramping.

Gentle movement like walking or stretching can also help blood flow and reduce pain. Staying hydrated and resting when you can makes a difference too.

If your pain is very severe or lasts more than a few days, try to reach a health worker or community clinic as soon as you are able.

Article 2 — All users

Category Health
Subcategory Period pain
Article Title Understanding period cramps
Article Content Period cramps (also called dysmenorrhoea) happen because your uterus contracts to shed its lining. This is completely normal.

The cramps usually start one to two days before your period begins and last two to three days. They are felt in the lower belly, lower back, or thighs.

Light exercise, rest, and staying warm can help. Over-the-counter pain relief such as ibuprofen or paracetamol can also reduce pain — always follow the dosage instructions on the packet.

Talk to a doctor or health worker if cramps are severe enough to stop you going to school or doing daily activities.
